# Basic Irregular Verbs

Unlike regular verbs that end in **-ed** in the past (like _liked_, _worked_), irregular verbs have special forms. You need to memorize them!

## Common A1 Irregular Verbs

Here are some of the most important ones:

### 1. Make (Hacer / Crear)

We use **make** when we create or produce something.

- **Infinitive**: Make
- **Past Simple**: Made
- **Past Participle**: Made

**Examples:**

- I **make** breakfast every morning. (Present)
- She **made** a cake yesterday. (Past)
- We have **made** a decision. (Perfect)

### 2. Do (Hacer / Realizar)

We use **do** for general activities, work, or tasks.

- **Infinitive**: Do
- **Past Simple**: Did
- **Past Participle**: Done

**Examples:**

- I **do** my homework.
- He **did** a great job.

### 3. Go (Ir)

- **Infinitive**: Go
- **Past Simple**: Went
- **Past Participle**: Gone

**Examples:**

- I **go** to school by bus.
- They **went** to the cinema last night.

### 4. Have (Tener)

- **Infinitive**: Have
- **Past Simple**: Had
- **Past Participle**: Had

**Examples:**

- I **have** a car.
- We **had** a great time at the party.
